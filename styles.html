Popular Dance Styles

Ballet:
A classical form characterized by precise movements, strength, and flexibility, often telling stories through choreography.
Indian Classical Dances:
Eight styles are recognized by the Sangeet Natak Academy, including Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu), Kathak (Uttar Pradesh), Kathakali (Kerala), Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh), Manipuri (Manipur), Mohiniyattam (Kerala), Odissi (Odisha), and Sattriya (Assam).

Modern and Contemporary Dance:


Modern Dance:
Emerging in the early 20th century, it emphasizes self-expression and unconventional movement, rejecting some of ballet's rigid structure. Contemporary Dance:
Combines elements from various styles, including modern, ballet, and jazz, allowing for creative exploration.
Folk and Ethnic Dance:
Folk Dance: Traditional dances passed down through generations, reflecting the cultural heritage of specific communities.
Irish Dance: Offers solo, ceili, and show dancing opportunities.
Belly Dance: Originating in the Middle East, characterized by intricate hip movements and shimmies.

Urban and Contemporary Styles:


Hip-Hop:
A dynamic street dance style with various forms like breaking, popping, and locking.
Jazz Dance:
Evolved from African American vernacular dance, characterized by energetic movements and improvisation.
Tap Dance:
Uses rhythmic footwork to create percussive sounds.
Swing Dance:
Encompasses various related dances like Lindy Hop and Balboa, known for their energetic and fun nature.
Other Dance Styles:
Ballroom Dance: A social and competitive dance style with various forms like waltz, tango, foxtrot, and cha-cha.
Latin Dance: Includes styles like salsa, bachata, merengue, and samba.
Broadway Jazz: Combines jazz dance with influences from musical theater. .
